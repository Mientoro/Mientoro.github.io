Leg and Suspension Team

The US military is developing autonomous search and rescue vehicles. The initial objective was to create a vehicle that is able to independently rotate each leg to use reaction forces in the wheel to self-stabilize mid air. This allows the vehicle to land at any orientation. To build off of this design, the next step was to create a suspension system that would protect the machine from hard landings, and allow it to overcome obstacles bigger than its wheels.

Our concept centered on a compressible spring suspension, that would use a pulley system to compress the spring. The gears operating the spring have a built in release mechanism via missing teeth that would allow the pulley to unspool at a specific interval. This system proved robust yet simple in design with less electrical parts and therefore a lower overall cost.
