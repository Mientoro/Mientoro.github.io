BattleBot

The idea of BattleBots always interested me. After learning of events near me, I decided to invest some time to tackle the challenge of creating a fairyweight bot. This weight class has a limit of 250 grams. This significant restriction was an interesting task to accomplish. Combined with the weight of the electronics, the chassis of the robot could not be very heavy. I experimented with a design that could house a spinning weapon, at the cost of a much weaker shell. After some deliberation, I decided on a new approach to a fairyweight bot that has not been used before.

Most BattleBots use only a few different approaches to a fight: pushing the opponent, flipping the opponent, or hitting them with a spinning weapon. My idea is to maximize the pushing power of my design. With all the competitors at the same light weight of 250 grams, I incorporated a drone motor and propeller to suction my bot to the floor. This effectively increases the friction of the tires on the floor and therefore the pushing power my vehicle has.
